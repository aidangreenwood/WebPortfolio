Final Info

In this final, we were tasked with designing a website and then taking someone else's plan and creating their website. The goal of this assignment was to practice working with someone else on a web development project. I thought it was really cool that the project was structured this way because it gave us the opportunity to experience both the client and developer roles.

Infiction

This was the site that I was assigned to build. InFiction is a website that allows individuals to post about different genres of fiction.

Donuts & Boba

This is the website that I designed for the final assignment. It is a website that lets users browse the menu of a restaurant that sells specialty donuts and boba drinks.

My Dev Experience

I think that the most difficult part of this assignment was knowing where to add content and how much of it to include. Personally, I think that the coding part of web design is pretty easy, but making sure I was fulfilling the client's expectations when it came to the actual content was a little stressful for me. I really enjoyed getting the chance to work with someone and trying to match their expectations for what they wanted their website to be. Just getting the opportunity to work with someone else in general was fun for me. I think that if I did this assignment again, I would simplify the site plan so that it only included the essentials for the first few stages of development, then update it as I went, because completing the entire site plan in one sitting was not very enjoyable. I definitely think that this project helped me understand how a real-world web development project might work. I also think that, because working with other people is such an important part of a career in web development, this project gave me a more realistic expectation of what working with a client would be like.

My Client Experience

The content and images for my page were inspired by the Crumbl Cookies website because I thought its layout was simple and easy to navigate. There were little to no problems when it came to communication. We both arrived to class and discussed ideas there, used text messages and Discord to send screenshots of our progress, and asked questions whenever we needed clarification. I would say that my developer definitely fulfilled my expectations for the site. I think that the only thing I would do differently is, right after the hiring process, sit down with my teammate and go over exactly what each of us would need to succeed in the project. That way, we would have had a clear outline of what we both wanted to accomplish during each stage of the project.
